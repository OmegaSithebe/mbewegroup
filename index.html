Company Law

"How to register a company in South Africa"

Fact: In South Africa, companies are registered through the Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (CIPC).

"What are the legal requirements for starting a business in South Africa?"

Fact: You need a company name, registration documents, and compliance with the Companies Act.


Human Rights & Labour Law

"How to report a human rights violation in South Africa"

Fact: Violations can be reported to the South African Human Rights Commission (SAHRC).

"What is considered unfair dismissal in South Africa?"

Fact: Unfair dismissal occurs when an employee is fired without a valid reason or proper procedure.

"How to file a labour dispute in South Africa"

Fact: Disputes can be filed with the Commission for Conciliation, Mediation, and Arbitration (CCMA).


Constitutional Law & General Practice

"How does the South African Constitution protect human rights?"

Fact: The Bill of Rights in the Constitution guarantees fundamental rights and freedoms.

"Where can I find affordable legal services in South Africa?"

Fact: Legal aid is available through the Legal Practice Council and Legal Aid South Africa.

"How much does it cost to hire a lawyer in South Africa?"

Fact: Costs vary depending on the complexity of the case and the lawyer's experience.


Criminal Law & RAF

"What should I do if I'm charged with a crime in South Africa?"

Fact: You have the right to remain silent and to consult with a criminal defense legal practioners.

"How do I claim from the Road Accident Fund in South Africa?"

Fact: You must submit a claim form with supporting documents, such as a police report and medical records.

A Story of Resilience & The Law

Understanding the complexities of the legal system through a remarkable story.

In a surprising and complex legal twist, the story of Ontlametse highlights the intricate nature of our justice system. Reports indicate that after her arrest, she defended herself in court and won a case against the state, navigating proceedings without formal legal training.

This situation underscores the critical importance of having qualified, experienced legal practitioners who understand the nuances of court procedure, evidence, and the law. While her story is one of remarkable personal resilience, officials have emphasized that practising law without proper qualifications remains illegal.

At MBEWE GROUP, we believe in empowering you with the right expertise. Our team ensures your rights are protected by professionals who have dedicated their lives to mastering the law, providing you with the certainty and confidence you need.
